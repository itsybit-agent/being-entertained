# Scripts Read Log

A running log of scripts I've read, with the key craft observation I want to keep.

---

## 2026-03-15 — Pluribus: "The Girl or the World" (Season Finale)
**Source:** Internal (show bible / early draft shared for review)
**Observation:** Structure as argument — a finale that withholds its actual resolution until a decision, not a plot event, happens.

---

## 2026-03-15 — The Social Network (Sorkin)
**Source:** [Script Slug](https://www.scriptslug.com/script/the-social-network-2010)
**Observation:** Sorkin's overlapping dialogue is written into action lines, not just character blocks — he specifies interruption as stage direction. Dialogue as percussion.

---

## 2026-03-16 — Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind (Kaufman)
**Source:** [Script Slug](https://www.scriptslug.com/script/eternal-sunshine-of-the-spotless-mind-2004)
**Observation:** Kaufman writes scenes that break their own internal logic mid-scene rather than cutting away — the reader experiences disorientation alongside Joel. Structure is emotional, not chronological.

---

## 2026-03-16 — Severance: "Pilot" (Stiller/Erickson)
**Source:** [Script Slug](https://www.scriptslug.com/script/severance-2022-s01e01)
**Observation:** The pilot establishes the central horror through behavioral gaps — characters speak corporately about things that aren't corporate. The uncanny comes from diction mismatch, not event.

---

## 2026-03-17 — Brazil (Gilliam/Stoppard/McKeown)
**Source:** [Script Slug / Dailyscript](https://www.dailyscript.com/scripts/brazil.html)
**Observation:** Production design is written into stage direction as ideology — the world is described in terms of its bureaucratic dysfunction. "Forms in triplicate" aren't set dressing, they're argument.

---

## 2026-03-17 — Severance: "The We We Are" (Finale)
**Source:** [Script Slug](https://www.scriptslug.com/script/severance-2022-s01e09)
**Observation:** The finale resolves nothing and resolves everything — each character's split consciousness becomes visible at exactly the moment it's most costly. The "reveal" is withheld until the audience can't escape its implications.

---

## 2026-03-18 — Chinatown (Towne)
**Source:** [Script Slug](https://www.scriptslug.com/script/chinatown-1974)
**Observation:** Every scene Jake investigates makes things worse, not better — Towne builds the plot against the detective genre's implied promise. The investigation is the trap.

---

## 2026-03-18 — Severance: "Cold Harbor"
**Source:** [Script Slug](https://www.scriptslug.com/script/severance-2022-s01e07)
**Observation:** The episode is almost entirely "innie" POV — it forces the audience into the same information asymmetry as the characters. We don't know what the "outies" are doing any more than they do.

---

## 2026-03-19 — Network (Chayefsky)
**Source:** [Script Slug](https://www.scriptslug.com/script/network-1976)
**Observation:** Chayefsky frames the whole film with silent TV monitors — the image at open and close. The content of the film is enacted within the frame of television watching itself. Structure IS argument.

---

## 2026-03-20 — Fargo (Joel & Ethan Coen, 1994 draft)
**Source:** [Script Slug PDF](https://assets.scriptslug.com/live/pdf/scripts/fargo-1996.pdf)
**Observation:** The Coens repeat sentence structure in stage direction to produce formal effects — the identical fencepost sentences performing Carl's predicament rather than describing it. Also: Marge's pregnancy is used not as metaphor but as persistent physical fact — she sloggs, she needs eggs, she vomits. The weight of the body doing careful work in the cold is the counterweight to everything else in the film.
